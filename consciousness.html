Maps of consciousness

A meditation on the spectrum of states — from contraction to expansion.

Many contemplative traditions describe consciousness as a spectrum of states we can move through. One well-known framing is David R. Hawkins' Map of Consciousness, which arranges states from the heavy, contracted emotions up toward the light, expansive ones. Used as a meditation, it becomes a way to notice where you are and gently incline toward openness.

The scale

A contemplative model, not a scientific measure — use it to locate and soften, not to judge.

A guided sitting

  1. Arrive. Settle into posture and take three slow "ohmmm" breaths.
  2. Locate. Without judgement, sense where you are on the map right now.
  3. Allow. Let whatever is present be fully felt — contraction included.
  4. Incline. With each out-breath, soften and open one notch toward acceptance, then love.
  5. Rest. Abide in spacious awareness — the witness behind every state.
"Letting go of the lower levels and choosing love, peace, and acceptance is itself the practice."
— after David R. Hawkins, Power vs. Force
