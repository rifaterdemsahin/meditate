Posture & position

The body holds the mind. A stable, relaxed posture is the foundation of a steady session.

The classic instruction is "relaxed but alert." You want a position you can hold comfortably for the whole session, with a spine that is upright without being rigid. Pick whichever of these fits your body — there is no single "correct" pose.

🪑 Chair

Feet flat on the floor, sitting toward the front of the seat, back self-supported and tall. Ideal for most people and for longer sits.

🧎 Cross-legged

On a cushion (zafu) with hips above the knees. Burmese, half-lotus, or full-lotus — choose the easiest that stays stable.

🧘 Kneeling (seiza)

Kneeling with a cushion or bench between the heels to take pressure off the knees and ankles.

🛏 Lying down

Flat on the back, arms slightly out, palms up (savasana). Great for body scans — just mind the tendency to fall asleep.

The seven-point checklist

  1. Seat — stable and grounded; hips slightly above knees if seated on the floor.
  2. Spine — upright and tall, following its natural curve, "like a stack of coins."
  3. Hands — resting on the thighs or in the lap, one cupped in the other.
  4. Shoulders — relaxed and slightly back, opening the chest.
  5. Chin — tucked very slightly, lengthening the back of the neck.
  6. Mouth & jaw — soft, lips gently closed, tongue resting on the roof of the mouth.
  7. Eyes — closed, or open with a soft downward gaze a few feet ahead.
