Common mistakes in meditation

Almost everyone hits these. Naming them is half the cure.

1. "I'm trying to stop thinking"

Meditation isn't about emptying the mind. Thoughts will keep coming.

Fix: The goal is to notice thoughts and return to your anchor — not to ban them.

2. Judging yourself for wandering

Beating yourself up for a wandering mind just adds a second layer of suffering.

Fix: Each time you notice you've drifted is the practice succeeding. Return kindly.

3. Chasing a special state

Expecting bliss or total calm sets you up to feel like a failure on ordinary days.

Fix: Drop expectations. Show up for whatever this session is, pleasant or not.

4. Forcing or straining

Gripping attention too tightly creates tension and headaches.

Fix: Hold attention lightly — "relaxed but alert." Soft effort, not white-knuckling.

5. Inconsistency

Long sessions once a week do far less than short sessions every day.

Fix: Five minutes daily beats an hour occasionally. Build the habit first.

6. Poor posture

Slumping invites drowsiness; rigidity invites pain.

Fix: Sit upright but relaxed — see Posture & Position.

7. Giving up too soon

The first weeks feel busy and awkward — many quit right before it clicks.

Fix: Commit to a few weeks. Restlessness early on is normal, not a verdict.

8. Only meditating when stressed

Treating it as an emergency tool means you never build the underlying skill.

Fix: Practise on calm days too, so the skill is there when you need it.
