Ohmmm breathing

Chant the primordial sound "Om" on a long exhale to settle the nervous system.

Om (pronounced A-U-M, "ohmmm") is the most well-known mantra in meditation. Chanting it lengthens the out-breath and produces a gentle vibration in the chest and skull that many find deeply calming. The technique below pairs a slow inhale with a long, humming "ohmmm" exhale.

How to practise

  1. Sit tall (see Posture & Position) and close your eyes.
  2. Breathe in slowly through the nose for about 4 seconds.
  3. On the out-breath, chant "ohmmm" — A...U...M — letting it fade as the breath empties (about 6–8 seconds).
  4. Feel the vibration. Pause briefly, then repeat for several rounds.
