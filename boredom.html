Boredom & flow go hand in hand

You have to pass through boredom — daily — to reach the zone of flow.

Boredom feels like a problem to escape. It isn't. Boredom is the doorway. When you sit with the restless, under-stimulated feeling instead of reaching for your phone, the mind settles, sharpens, and eventually slips into flow — that absorbed, timeless state where you and the activity become one. Meditation is daily practice at staying in the doorway long enough to walk through it.

Why they belong together

  • Boredom precedes depth. The first minutes of any focused effort feel dull. Flow lives on the other side of that dullness.
  • Distraction is the escape hatch. Every time you flee boredom with a quick hit of stimulation, you abandon the path to flow.
  • Tolerance is trainable. Meditation builds the capacity to sit with "nothing happening" — the exact skill flow requires.
  • It must be daily. Like any capacity, the tolerance for boredom (and the access to flow) fades without regular practice.

From boredom to the zone

1. Notice the urge

Feel boredom arise and the itch to escape it.

2. Stay put

Don't reach for stimulation. Breathe and remain.

3. Soften

The restlessness peaks, then settles into spaciousness.

4. Absorption

Attention narrows and deepens — flow begins.

"The best moments usually occur when a person's body or mind is stretched to its limits in a voluntary effort to accomplish something difficult and worthwhile."
— Mihály Csíkszentmihályi, Flow

This is the second B of the PABB goal in practice — boredom, tolerated daily, becomes the trigger for creativity and flow.
